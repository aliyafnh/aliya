Choosing yourself isn’t selfish. It’s the moment you stop abandoning yourself to keep others comfortable.

By Aliya • 1.1.2026

“The softest flex is staying loyal to yourself.”

Read it slowly. Let it land.

THE SHIFT

The day I stopped negotiating my needs

I used to call self-abandonment “being easy to love”. I used to soften my boundaries so nobody would feel guilty. But peace doesn’t come from being low-maintenance. It comes from being honest.

Choosing yourself is choosing the version of you that survives long-term.

The truth is: when you keep shrinking, people don’t learn to respect you, they just learn what you’ll tolerate.

I stopped

over-explaining.

I started

choosing clarity.

I kept

my peace.

BOUNDARIES

What choosing myself looked like

  • Saying “no” without adding a paragraph.
  • Leaving when the pattern repeats, not when it becomes unbearable.
  • Not chasing closure from people who enjoy confusion.
  • Being consistent with my standards, even when I miss them.

It wasn’t dramatic. It was quiet. And that’s how I knew it was real.

You can love people and still choose yourself first.
